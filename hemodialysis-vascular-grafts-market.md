<p><p>The Hemodialysis Vascular Grafts market consists of various materials used to create artificial blood vessels for dialysis patients. Polyester grafts are durable and commonly used but may have limited biocompatibility. Polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE) offers excellent biocompatibility and is resistant to thrombosis, making it a popular choice. Polyurethane grafts provide flexibility and strength, enhancing patient comfort. Biological materials, often derived from natural sources, aim to mimic the body’s own tissues, promoting better integration and reducing complications associated with synthetic materials.</p></p>
